The problem

Today, 1.7 billion people are affected by health issues related to glucose: a continuum of health problems ranging from mild insulin resistance and overweight, to obesity and diabetes.

In a few years, half a billion people will be affected by full blown diabetes and 1 billion by pre-diabetes.
This is the single biggest chronic disease in the world, which, along with smoking, causes nearly all the major health burdens of the 21st century, including heart disease, stroke, dementia and even cancer.

The cause of this sugar epidemic is in our diet: we develop a sugar addiction because 80% of the food we buy at the grocery store hides some sort of sugar. We are not aware of this addiction and its deadly consequences and are unprepared to deal with it; even worse, doctors are not expected, trained, or paid to diagnose or treat it.

Read more... The 2014 documentary film Fed up is poised to be the Inconvenient truth of the health movement.
Featuring Bill Clinton along with well known doctors and researchers, it casts light on the sugar epidemic and its causes, building on the idea that the solution won't come by government or by Big Food. Instead, it will require a grassroots, bottom-up effort on the part of families and savvy consumers.
We as technologists and doctors must lead the way.

The Solution

Cinnamon breaks the sugar addiction cycle, and makes it possible to prevent health issues caused by high glucose levels, and even revert the diseases already developed.

It builds awareness by keeping blood sugar checked at all times and helps remain within a safe and steady range. Through continuous feedback, personalised tips and a unique gardening interface, it also helps achieving a diet mostly based on plants and real, healthy food.

We want to focus on the existing 350 million diabetics first, because they already have a clear and urgent unmet need; then, we want to address the over 1 billion people with health issues related to glucose.




The computer vision

The world is full of glucose meters and most of them are not so well connected in the internet of our things.

We designed a process that takes advantage of your phone's camera and magically integrates any bgm on the market.

Even though we envision a revolutionary device that will change the way we track glucose in our body we need to face the actual challenge.
